What we are

YourKitchen is a care-coordination platform that mobilizes a person's community to put meals on the table during life's hardest seasons — a new baby, an illness, a deployment, a loss. The recipient creates a "kitchen"; the people who love them claim dates, choose restaurant meals, and pay, with delivery through the DoorDash and Uber networks and a one-tap text confirmation before any card is charged. Meal-train tools have existed for years, but they stop at a sign-up sheet — YourKitchen moves the money and the food, earning a take rate on every meal plus an optional family membership. It compounds the way marketplaces do: the more of someone's village shows up, the stickier the kitchen, and the hard seasons that drive demand recur across a lifetime. The meal is the visible transaction; coordinating the village is the moat.

Investor / category line

YourKitchen turns a community into a care team — and earns a take rate on every meal it delivers. It's the transactional layer the old meal-train sign-up sheets never built.

Payer / VA / Food-as-Medicine

For health plans and the VA, YourKitchen is a Food-as-Medicine and SDOH benefit that activates a member's own support network to deliver nutritious meals during the highest-risk windows — postpartum, post-discharge, active treatment, bereavement. Because the meals come from people the member loves rather than a vendor, engagement and average order value run well above standard meal-delivery benefits — turning a covered benefit into one members actually use.

Why they matter

Read the business like a marketplace, not an app

Your instinct to ask "SaaS or app?" matters here: the answer dictates which numbers you live by. YourKitchen is a transactional platform, so the scoreboard is marketplace-shaped, not subscription-shaped.

GMV × take rateThe engine
Revenue scales with meals delivered, not seats sold. Track GMV as the north star and take-rate dollars as what you keep. Care+ MRR is real but secondary — leading with it would undersell the larger lever.
AOVThe differentiator
A ~40%-above-market order value is rare and defensible — it means more take-rate dollars per order and a stronger payer story (better benefit utilization). Protect it; it's a headline, not a footnote.
Activation · repeat · village depthThe moat signal
Supporters per kitchen, activation, and repeat are how you prove network effects are real. They turn "nice idea" into "compounding platform" in a diligence conversation.
LTV : CAC · NRRThe efficiency
These show the model is capital-efficient and compounding across recurring life events — exactly what makes a marketplace (and a covered benefit) fundable.
The playbook

Moving the numbers

Grow GMV
Four independent dials: more activated kitchens, more supporters per kitchen (village depth), more meals per kitchen (longer calendars), and higher AOV. Each can move without the others.
Lift activation
Make the first paid send effortless and make sure it actually arrives — reliable dispatch and a tip that gets picked up is activation work. Celebrate the first delivery; it's the moment a kitchen becomes real.
Raise AOV
Multi-meal and family-size sends, "gift a week," premium restaurants, and add-ons. Nudge the village toward generosity at the moment of giving, when intent is highest.
Protect margin
Make sure the courier + service fee you collect fully covers your real fulfillment cost (incl. the courier markup). A take rate that quietly leaks on every delivery caps the whole model.
Retention

Minimizing churn (the honest version)

Churn is the metric most likely to mislead you here, because a kitchen ending is not the same as a customer leaving. Separate the two:

Subscription churnReduce
Care+ cancels. Counter it with lasting value beyond the event — the keepsake/archive, the annual plan, and the Founding Member 3-year term all extend the relationship past the hard season.
Kitchen lifecycleExpected
A kitchen winding down when the baby's older or treatment ends is natural, not failure. The goal isn't to keep it open forever — it's to bring that family and village back into the network for the next season.
Involuntary churnEliminate
Failed payments and undelivered texts churn people silently. Fixing card retries and SMS deliverability is retention work disguised as plumbing — every dropped confirmation is a lost order and a frustrated family.
The metric that actually matters

Not "did this kitchen stay open forever" but "does this person re-enter the network" — as a recipient again, or as an organizer who starts a kitchen for someone else. Track network re-entry, and every ended kitchen becomes a seed rather than a loss.

Distribution

Growing organically

Your single biggest structural advantage: every kitchen is an invite loop. One recipient surfaces an entire village; each supporter experiences the product first-hand; some become organizers for the next person who needs it. That's your viral coefficient — built into the core action, not bolted on.

Coordinator → Organizer
The flywheel's center. Instrument it, prompt it ("Know someone whose village is ready?"), and make starting a kitchen one tap from inside the coordinator experience, right after a delivery lands well.
Share surface
Kitchen links and share cards already turn every send into distribution. Keep the shared artifact beautiful and personal — it's your most credible ad, made by the people who use you.
Community channels
Highest-leverage organic: postpartum, military-spouse, caregiver, and college-parent groups, plus referrers who sit at the moment of need — hospital social workers, doulas, lactation consultants, faith care ministries.
Build-in-public
The founder narrative compounds an audience that converts warm and cheap. Keep documenting real traction; it's CAC you only pay once.
Gifting & partners
"Gift Care+" turns members into acquirers. Restaurant, doula, and employer partnerships seed new villages — and the employer door is also the on-ramp to your B2B2C payer motion.
Measure it

Watch your K-factor and the share of new kitchens sourced from prior villages. When that share climbs, growth is compounding on its own — and your CAC story writes itself.
